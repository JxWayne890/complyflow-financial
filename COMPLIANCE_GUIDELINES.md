# Compliance Guidelines for Content Creation

This document outlines the compliance rules, workflows, and best practices for creating marketing content for the RIA. It incorporates the SEC Marketing Rule and specific client protocols provided by Riley Johnston.

## 1. Project Objective & Scope
**Goal**: Create approved, educational, and compliant content for:
*   **Channels**: Website Blog, LinkedIn (Firm & Personal Pages).
*   **Topics**: Investment education, exit planning, wealth management, alternative investments, business sales, valuation, and economic commentary.
*   **Regulators**: SEC (Federal), State Regulators, Internal CCO.

## 2. Target Audiences & Permissible Topics
*All drafts must clearly identify the intended audience in the header.*

| Audience Type | Description | Permissible Topics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **General Public** | Unlicensed public audience (prospects, retail clients). | General financial education, business exit planning, retirement education, behavioral finance, market commentary. **NO specific recommendations.** |
| **Accredited Investors** | Sophisticated investors meeting accredited definitions. | Higher-level investment discussions, basic alternatives education, private offerings overview. **NO specific offers.** |
| **Qualified Purchasers** | High-net-worth clients eligible for specific private offerings. | Advanced alternative investment commentary, private placement education. **NO specific offers.** |

## 3. Content Creation Workflow
1.  **Drafting**: Write in plain language. Cite sources. Add disclaimers. Avoid superlatives.
2.  **Internal Review**: Reviewed by Subject Matter Expert (Advisor, CIO, or Principal).
3.  **Compliance Submission**: Submit package (Draft + Audience + Sources + Disclaimers + Author/Date).
4.  **CCO Review**: CCO reviews for compliance, requests edits, or approves. (**5 business days lead time**; Rush requires pre-approval).
5.  **Archiving**: Approved versions saved in compliance archive.

## 4. Language Guidance
**Principle**: "Avoid Sith Language" (Absolutes). Never imply guaranteed outcomes.

| Do ✅ | Avoid ❌ |
| :--- | :--- |
| Use **"may", "could", "might"** | Avoid **"will", "guaranteed", "certain"** |
| Focus on **education** | Avoid **product promotion** (without filing) |
| "Past performance is **not indicative**..." | Avoid implying **performance certainty** |
| Use **factual, balanced data** | Avoid **one-sided** presentations |
| Use **specific audience disclaimers** | Avoid **unclear** targeting |
| "Tax-Efficient", "Optimize", "Enhance" | "Tax-Proof", "Full", "Complete", "Always" |

## 5. Standard Disclaimers
*Must be included based on the target audience.*

**General Public**
> "This material is for informational purposes only and does not constitute investment advice, a recommendation, or an offer of any security. Please consult your financial advisor before making any financial decisions."

**Accredited / Qualified**
> "This material is intended for accredited and qualified investors. Investments in private offerings involve a high degree of risk and may not be suitable for all investors. Past performance is not indicative of future results."

## 6. Prohibited & Sample Content

### ✅ Sample Content Types Allowed
*   Educational blog posts & Market commentary (balanced).
*   Exit planning, Estate & succession planning topics.
*   Tax planning strategies (general only).
*   Wealth transfer case studies (anonymous, illustrative).

### ❌ Prohibited Content
*   **Testimonials/Endorsements**: Unless strictly following SEC rules (see Section 7).
*   **Unsubstantiated claims**: Any performance claim without proof.
*   **"Salesy" pitches**: Aggressive product promotion.
*   **Specific Recommendations**: To the general public.
*   **Hypothetical Performance**: Never on public websites.

## 7. SEC Marketing Rule: Specific Activity Rules

### Testimonials & Endorsements
*   **Disclosures**: Must disclose if Client/Non-Client, Compensation (Cash/Non-Cash), and Conflicts of Interest.
*   **Agreements**: Written agreement required for compensated promoters.

### Third-Party Ratings
*   **Disclosures**: Date of rating, Identity of rater, Compensation paid.
*   **Due Diligence**: Must verify rating was unbiased.

### Performance Advertising
*   **Gross vs. Net**: Net performance must be shown with equal prominence to gross.
*   **Time Periods**: 1, 5, and 10-year periods required.
*   **Substantiation**: *If you can't prove it, don't say it.*

## 8. Archiving & Documentation
All final versions must be stored in the digital compliance archive tagged with:
*   Author
*   Date
*   Audience
*   CCO Approval Date

## ✅ Pre-Publication Checklist
- [ ] **Audience Defined**: Is the intended audience (General/Accredited/Qualified) listed in the header?
- [ ] **Absolutes Removed**: Checked for "will", "always", "guaranteed", "full", "tax-proof"?
- [ ] **Disclaimer Added**: Is the correct disclaimer (General vs. Accredited) included?
- [ ] **Substantiated**: Are all statistics cited?
- [ ] **Balanced**: Are risks mentioned alongside benefits?
- [ ] **Timeline**: Submitted 5 days before publication?
